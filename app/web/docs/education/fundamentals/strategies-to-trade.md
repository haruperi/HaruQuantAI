
# Strategies to Trade

Trading strategies can be classified by their approach to market direction and timeframe. Understanding strategy categories helps in portfolio construction and diversification.

## Directional

Directional strategies profit from price movement in a specific direction.

### With the Trend

Strategies that align with the prevailing market direction.

#### Trend Following

Captures sustained directional moves by entering after trend establishment and staying until trend reverses.

**Characteristics:**
- Low win rate (35-45%), high reward-to-risk (3:1 to 10:1)
- Longest holding periods - days to months
- Performs well in trending markets, struggles in range-bound conditions
- Most profits from a few large winners

**Common Indicators:** Moving average crossovers, Donchian channels, ADX, Parabolic SAR

#### Momentum

Exploits continuation of strong price movements over shorter periods.

**Characteristics:**
- Medium win rate (45-55%), moderate reward-to-risk (1.5:1 to 3:1)
- Shorter holding period than trend following - hours to days
- Focuses on relative strength and price acceleration
- Works well after breakouts or strong moves

**Common Indicators:** RSI above/below 50, MACD, Rate of Change, Relative Strength vs index

#### Bias

Exploits known market biases or inefficiencies.

**Examples:**
- Bullish bias in equities over long term
- Contango/backwardation in futures
- Day of week patterns (Monday effect, Friday effect)
- Time of day patterns (opening range breakouts, lunch lull)

#### Intermarket

Uses relationships between different markets to identify trend opportunities.

**Examples:**
- Bond prices up -> Dollar down (inverse relationship)
- Gold and Dollar typically inverse
- Energy sector stocks follow crude oil
- Sector rotation strategies

#### Seasonal

Exploits recurring seasonal patterns based on calendar effects.
**Intra-day:** Morning strength, lunch weakness, closing rally patterns
**Daily**: Day of week patterns (Monday gap, Friday afternoon)
**Monthly**: "Sell in May and go away," Santa Claus rally, January effect, End-of-month buying
**Commodity Seasonals:**
- Agricultural commodities follow planting/harvest cycles
- Natural gas peaks in winter (heating) and summer (cooling)
- Gasoline peaks in summer driving season

#### Breakout

Enters when price breaks through defined support/resistance levels.

**Types:**
- Range breakout (Donchian channel, volatility squeeze)
- Resistance/support breakout
- Opening range breakout (ORB)
- Pattern breakout (triangle, flag, consolidation)

### Against the Trend

Strategies that profit from reversals or price returning to mean.

#### Mean Reversion

Buys oversold conditions and sells overbought conditions, expecting price to return to average.

**Characteristics:**
- High win rate (55-70%), low reward-to-risk (1:1 to 1.5:1)
- Short holding periods - minutes to days
- Profits from price oscillation
- Vulnerable to strong trends - can have catastrophic losses if unchecked

**Common Indicators:** RSI extremes (&lt;30, &gt;70), Bollinger Bands (2-3 SD), Z-score, Stochastic

**Critical Requirements:**
- Must have strict stop losses
- Works best in range-bound markets
- Should avoid trending markets (use filters)

#### Counter Trend

Trades against prevailing trend looking for reversals or exhaustion.

**Approach:**
- Identifies trend exhaustion signals
- Looks for divergences (price vs indicator)
- Uses overbought/oversold with reversal confirmation
- High risk - "picking tops and bottoms"

**Warning:** "The trend is your friend until the end" - counter-trend trading is difficult and should be approached with caution. Requires tight risk management.

#### Failed Breakout

Fades failed breakouts - enters opposite direction when breakout fails to follow through.

**Logic:**
- Price breaks resistance but quickly reverses back below
- Stop losses from breakout traders create momentum opposite direction
- "Bull trap" or "bear trap" patterns

#### Intermarket

Uses market divergences to identify reversal opportunities.
- Gold rallying while Dollar strong (divergence, potential reversal)
- Sector underperformance reverting to mean

#### Bias

Exploits reversion of temporary biases.
- Friday afternoon weakness reversing Monday morning
- Overnight gap fades
- Opening range fades

#### Seasonal

Fades seasonal extremes expecting mean reversion.
**Intra-day:** Fade opening gaps, fade end-of-day extremes
**Daily:** Monday gap fades, Friday reversals
**Monthly:** End of strong seasonal period reversals

## Time Frame

Strategies can be classified by holding period.

### Intraday

All positions closed before market close. No overnight risk.

**Advantages:**
- No overnight gap risk
- High trade frequency provides better statistics
- Daily reset - fresh start each day
- Lower margin requirements

**Disadvantages:**
- Higher commission costs (more trades)
- More time-intensive - requires monitoring
- Limited profit potential per trade
- Noise can be higher on shorter timeframes

### Intraweek

Positions held 1-5 days. Swing trading timeframe.

**Advantages:**
- Captures multi-day moves
- Lower commission impact
- Less monitoring required
- Good balance between activity and noise filtering

**Considerations:**
- Weekend gap risk
- Overnight news risk
- Requires overnight margin

### Intramonth

Positions held 1-4 weeks. Position trading timeframe.

**Advantages:**
- Captures larger trends
- Minimal monitoring required
- Very low commission impact
- Lower stress - not watching tick-by-tick

**Considerations:**
- Lower trade frequency - requires patience
- Greater capital tied up per position
- Multiple overnight and weekend gaps
- May experience significant intra-position drawdowns
